How industry-wide effort helps

Surveying problematic for safe autonomous driving situations is a hard problem. Industry addresses this problem with multi-billion dollar investments into fleets of vehicles, safety drivers, engineers and researchers. One of the weaknesses of the industry is that the datasets assembled in this way are expensive, incomplete with no data sharing mechanism available to the participants. Complementing this approach with a public, nonprofit, crowdsourced dataset to map 'unknowns' and 'unknown unknowns' of the hard examples of autonomous driving might create such a mechanism and become a focal point of collaboration in the industry. Our contributors are individuals and companies who care about autonomous vehicles safety. Please feel free to contribute in any way possible - feedback, tweets, pull requests, donations.

3D Illusuion

This has a potential of confusing an autonomous vehicle, because the monocular sensor image may look indistinguishable from a 3D object. The severity is relatively low, bacause at worst an autonomous vehicle will stop and block the traffic.

An unusual pose of the vehicle

Unusual pose of a vehicle on a road might confuse an automonous vehicle. The severity of this example is high, because a misinterpretation of the object has a potential of creating an accudent.
